I also have Charcot Arthropathy, which causes softening of the bones in the ankle. With that said, my career path in nursing was kinda put on hold. I am currently trying to fix my foot and get on the kidney transplant list. It is easier said than done as you have to meet certain requirements that I, as of now, have not. At the moment I am out of work, it was hard to work anyway with a foot that hurts to walk on and having zero energy from low hemoglobin with dialysis. It really sucks not being healthy like I used to be. I guess I have always been over weight, but until I got kidney disease things were looking up. I had a great career, I was losing weight, and it seemed that my future was bright. Then I started having less energy, I started gaining weight again. I finally went back to my doctors and got the bad news about my kidneys.

Life goes on though. I got a kid to support and when I get healthy again I would like to have money and a job, so I started looking into computer science.
